Gap Analysis
Where Structure Breaks Down

The RACI defines 12 production stages with explicit ownership, handoffs, and quality gates. Here's where the current Asana structure doesn't support that.

GapImpactSeverity
No intake lane distinction — Strategic, Operational, and Rapid Support requests all enter the same queue MSDs can't prioritize strategy-level work from routine asset requests High
Kanban has 3 columns, workflow has 12 stages — "In Progress" covers Stages 3–11 No visibility into where a campaign actually is High
No quality gates — Gate 6.5, 9.5, and 10.5 have no structural equivalent Work advances without verification. Assets reach distribution without creative review. High

Signal Gate Flow
Signal Received → MSD Routes → Container Built → Capacity Confirmed → Sprint Entry

Every request passes through all five steps. No exceptions. Campaign Container must exist before capacity check — you can't confirm bandwidth without knowing the deliverables.

5 Intake Governance Rules

5 rules

1. No direct requests — all work enters through the intake form.

2. Strategy enables work — MSDs route before PMs execute.

3. Capacity check required — Ops Lead reviews bandwidth using container deliverables. If tight, Ops flags and team decides trade-offs — not a hard stop.

4. Distribution requires a container — no channel output without a campaign container on the Kanban Board.

5. Bypass triggers escalation — any skip of the Signal Gate is flagged and escalated to Exec Dir.

Handoff Model
Intake → Kanban Bridge

The Campaign Container is built during intake — before capacity check. You can't confirm bandwidth without knowing the deliverables. Once capacity clears, the container multi-homes to Kanban and enters Brief Creation.

MSD Routes → Container Built → Capacity Confirmed → Sprint Entry → Kanban: Brief Creation

Lane 1 — Strategic

Full Campaign Container with all 22 subtasks, dependencies, and milestone gates. PM builds container from the MSD-routed brief; Ops Lead uses the deliverable list to confirm capacity. Container multi-homes to Kanban at Sprint Entry.

Lane 2 — Operational

Lightweight Campaign Container. Skips Stages 3–4 (brief/direction already known). PM builds container before capacity check. Starts at Pre-Production or Production on Kanban.

Lane 3 — Rapid Support

No Campaign Container. Stays in Intake project. PM assigns directly to relevant specialist. 24–72hr turnaround.

3/26 Meeting Decisions
Resolved

Decisions captured from the 3/26 ops meeting with Kaylin, Eric, and Cheyenne.

✓ Multi-homing Confirmed

Single source of truth. Multi-home tasks across projects. Use saved views to manage clutter.

✓ 3 Board Views, Not 13 Columns

One Kanban project, three filtered tabs: Production Pipeline, Review & Approval, Distribution & Measurement. Stages tagged, not columned.

✓ Campaign Container Moves Earlier

Container built before capacity check — Ops Lead needs deliverables to confirm bandwidth. Brief drives the container; container drives the capacity check.

✓ Gate Enforcement = Hybrid

Asana rules handle automation. PMs verify gates in real-time — not a weekly batch. Creative review bottleneck acknowledged; Sr. Graphic Designer to handle more first-pass reviews.

✓ Migrate, Don't Grandfather

Existing work moves to the new structure. "Rip the band-aid off." Grandfathering would compound the same problems across future sprints.

✓ Capacity = Check, Not Planning

Kaylin's role is guiding capacity constraints to MSDs before strategy — not making deliverable-level decisions. MSDs strategize within the framework of confirmed capacity.
